## The signal that makes this a QSO

RWJBarnabas Health — the parent — has been **publicly active on WPV** since 2022. The system published a "5 Steps" framework with HealthLeaders Media ([HealthLeaders article](https://www.healthleadersmedia.com/clinical-care/5-steps-rwjbarnabas-took-combat-workplace-violence)) covering: visible security presence, de-escalation training, behavioral-health response teams, EMR flagging of high-risk patients, and post-incident debriefs. So the system has a stated WPV program — but it's a **playbook**, not infrastructure.

Clara Maass specifically: 500+ nurses unionized with 1199SEIU in 2022; safe staffing has been a contract issue ever since. RWJBarnabas filed (and was filed against) at NLRB through 2024 ([Belleville Patch](https://patch.com/new-jersey/belleville/labor-strife-flares-again-clara-maass-hospital-belleville)). The nurses' safety claim is now contract-bargaining leverage, not a general grievance.

NJ's N.J.S.A. 26:2H-5.17 is In force — it requires every healthcare facility to have a documented WPV program with annual risk assessments. RWJBarnabas has the program on paper; the question is whether Clara Maass's BH unit and ED have the **physical infrastructure** the law presumes.
